About

Hello. I'm Joshua Lawson and this is my landing page. Here is a little about me:

I tried to fight it. Tried to pretend it wasn't there. But it was, all the time, haunting me.

It started when I was young. The internet was just becoming a "thing". Thousands of people were signing into chat rooms preceded by that awful dial-up tone. I would help my neighbors and family members with their computers. "You're so good with computers!” they would exclaim. And I felt like a wizard. It seemed natural to me, but people were in awe of my ability. My career path, it seemed, was obvious.

But disaster struck teenage Josh when he came against an obstacle he was not mature enough to overcome: failure. That's right; I failed one of my certification tests.

I have grown a lot in the intervening years, but teenage Josh took his failure as a sign that he was not cut out to be in the tech industry, as he once believed. And so, with a heavy heart, I put away my pocket protector and put on my work boots. What followed was a series of menial jobs, each slightly better than the last, but never fulfilling.

When it came time to shift gears again, I asked myself what I truly wanted to do. The answer was simple; I loved technology. I wanted to be around it.

So I chose to pursue a career in Front End Web Development. I enrolled at The Iron Yard and embarked upon one of the most challenging and rewarding things I've ever done. And though I’m excited for what the future now holds, I wouldn't call all those other jobs a waste. On the contrary, I learned a lot about myself and what I can accomplish, about working for companies and what they expect, about working with clients and making them happy, and most of all, about working with people.

My work history to this point shows that I can do almost anything and my education shows that I can learn almost anything, quickly. I look forward to applying this to my future endeavors.
