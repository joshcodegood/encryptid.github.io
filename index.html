A little about me:

Some things I love...

I have always loved technology. Nary a gadget within 10 feet of me was left unfiddled. By sheer providence, the internet and I came of age at around the same time, so of course the stage was set for me to become one of the great front end developers!

Instead, I learned some HTML and took the next decade off, technology-wise.

What I did next was a lot of work that I found unfulfilling in an attempt to make a living. I often found myself frustrated and bored. I am a lifelong learner, and my work just wasn’t giving me what I needed. I had to do some soul-searching; I tried to get at the source of what was missing in my life.

I thought back to high school, when I had elected to take a certification course in Cisco networking. Working with computers was the one thing I had always been naturally good at; I thought it was going to be a slam dunk!

It was not. What can I say, the exam was hard! But the exam was nowhere near as hard as how I internalized that experience, because that was the moment I lost faith in my technical ability.

Looking back, of course, that was a complete overreaction. You experience a failure, you try to learn from it, and you get back on the horse and you teach that dumb horse a lesson!

The journey up to that point was by no means wasted, however. I learned a lot about myself; I discovered that I am a natural leader and that I enjoy helping others be their best. I’m also dedicated to the point of stubborness. I genuinely enjoy the results that only true collaboration can bring.

So, I view part of my journey as putting things back to the way they should have been all along. I went to The Iron Yard (RIP) to learn how to code. I had a lot of catching up to do, but I’m happy to be in a career where learning is always rewarded!
